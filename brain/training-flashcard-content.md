# Training Flashcard Content (short-form, video-specific)

Source: Brayden's transcripts of the 8 locked videos (see [[training-videos]]). All content is original paraphrase, not verbatim transcript text. 6 cards per video × 8 videos = 48 cards.

**Format rule (2026-06-30):** front = short cue/term (a few words, not a full question), back = short phrase (not a full sentence). Optimized for memorization/recall, not explanation — that's what Final Exam is for. Previous version of this file used full Q&A sentences; Brayden flagged it as too hard to remember, replaced below.

## Category 1 — AI RECEPTIONIST (Video 1)

1. Front: Missed call math → Back: Calls missed/day × ticket price × days/yr
2. Front: AI receptionist → Back: Human-sounding AI phone agent
3. Front: Templated vs custom → Back: Templated = plug-in generic; custom = agency-built, tailored
4. Front: Full AI vs overflow → Back: 24/7 AI, or AI only when human can't answer
5. Front: Voicemail problem → Back: Callers don't leave one — they call a competitor
6. Front: Pricing model → Back: ~$0.12/min raw; agencies charge flat rate + overage

## Category 2 — TONALITY (Video 2)

1. Front: First 4 seconds → Back: Sharp, enthusiastic, expert
2. Front: 90% of communication → Back: Tonality + body language (words = 10%)
3. Front: Charisma → Back: Learnable — tonality + body language + no dumb words
4. Front: Rapport = → Back: They feel you care + you're like them
5. Front: Why use tonality → Back: Forces listener to engage, not tune out
6. Front: Flat tone risk → Back: People mentally check out

## Category 3 — DISCOVERY (Video 3)

1. Front: "How do you mean by that?" → Back: Pushes past the surface answer
2. Front: Repeat their word → Back: Echo their emotion word to open them up
3. Front: "How long has this been going on?" → Back: Makes them relive the pain
4. Front: Pause after a question → Back: Forces a deeper, real answer
5. Front: Good discovery question → Back: "What's causing this?" / "Why now?"
6. Front: Why discover before pitching → Back: Surfaced pain = urgency

## Category 4 — GATEKEEPER (Video 4)

1. Front: Real goal w/ gatekeeper → Back: Get to the decision-maker
2. Front: Skip the formal intro → Back: Sounds like a sales call, gets screened
3. Front: Tone with gatekeeper → Back: Casual, like you already know them
4. Front: After you ask → Back: Pause — let them respond
5. Front: Over-explaining → Back: Reads as nervous/salesy
6. Front: Casual > professional → Back: Polished = guarded; casual = safe

## Category 5 — OBJECTIONS (Video 5)

1. Front: "I'll think about it" → Back: No urgency — stuck in a frame
2. Front: 2 drivers of change → Back: Pain + fear of future pain
3. Front: Consequence question → Back: "Then what happens?" — chain it deeper
4. Front: Identity frame → Back: Contrast them vs. people who stayed stuck
5. Front: Concerned tone → Back: Seeds doubt, lowers their guard
6. Front: Rebuttal mistake → Back: Argues their frame instead of reframing

## Category 6 — QUALIFYING (Video 6)

1. Front: Qualify on money → Back: Household income should cover the payment
2. Front: Debt ≠ disqualified → Back: Fine if income covers it + product pays for itself
3. Front: Qualifying approach → Back: Ask like a coach, not an interrogator
4. Front: Can't afford it → Back: Qualify out fast, move on
5. Front: Why move fast → Back: Keeps overall call efficiency high
6. Front: Ask about money history → Back: Shows if they invest in solving problems

## Category 7 — BOOKING (Video 7)

1. Front: Before calling back → Back: Pull up their info, don't re-ask
2. Front: Pushy gap → Back: Prospects see it more than setters do
3. Front: When to get assertive → Back: After rapport + value + qualifying
4. Front: "Got 2 minutes?" → Back: Small ask = easy yes
5. Front: Assume the appointment → Back: Don't ask "want to move forward?"
6. Front: Best time to ask for referral → Back: When they're thanking you

## Category 8 — TIME MANAGEMENT (Video 8)

1. Front: #1 reason reps miss numbers → Back: No structure, just reacting
2. Front: Best call block → Back: Morning — higher connect rates
3. Front: Sourcing block → Back: Builds tomorrow's pipeline
4. Front: Lunch passive learning → Back: Listen to top calls, low effort
5. Front: End-of-day reflection → Back: Turns activity into improvement
6. Front: Core mindset → Back: Work intentional, not longer
