## How I Sell AI Receptionists to Local Businesses with Cold Calling (COMPLETE SYSTEM)

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v1piqxyWJvM
Channel: SixFlow Automations | Published: 2026-05-02 | Length: 14:34

### Key patterns observed

**Script structure used:**
1. "Is this [Business]? Is this the owner?" → confirm decision-maker first
2. Hook: establish credibility + bold claim ("free AI audit") + pause
3. Neutral response → "Is this something you've come across before?" → get implicit permission to explain
4. Explain + urgency + ask: "Does a 15-minute call sound fair sometime when you're available?"
5. Loop back to the same ask regardless of objection path

**Key insight — the ask loop:** "The more times you can ask in your calls, the more success you're going to have." Every objection path circles back to the same close. Don't treat objections as dead ends — each one is a detour back to the booking ask.

**Show rate system:**
- 3 follow-ups before the meeting: 24h before, 1h before, 15 min before
- Personalized Loom video right after booking (1–2 min, reference their name + one thing from the call) — biggest show-rate lever in the system

**Close meeting (Nate's call):**
- Skip chit-chat, get to business fast
- Ask "is there anything in particular you wanted to discuss?" — shows you're not wasting their time
- Slide deck with prepared materials — controls the conversation

### Raw transcript

This simple system books me meetings every day and it's how I make thousands a month selling AI. This cold calling system is the one I use today because it is an absolute machine for booking meetings.

I get businesses from Google Maps, I transfer them to a spreadsheet, and that's my lead list.

I start off every call saying, "Hi, is this X business? Is this John's Plumbing?" They're going to be like, "Yes." Then I ask, "Is this the owner?" They're going to say yes or no. If they say yes, I move on to the hook. If they say no, I don't waste my time with people who aren't decision-makers, so I'll simply end the call politely.

My hook is this: "Hey, this is Adam calling from SixFlow. I'm not sure if you've heard our name tossed around, but we work with home service businesses across Ontario. I'm actually the founder here. We're offering free AI audits to home service business owners right now." Boom, bold claim, captured interest and curiosity, and I'll just pause there.

At this point, they can do one of three things. The first thing they can do is hang up in your face. The second possible thing is they're going to hit you with a neutral response — something like "Okay, uh, what does that mean?" In which case you ask them, "Is this something you've come across before?" Most of the time, they're going to say no or not really. And once they say, "No, I haven't heard of this before," it's implicit permission for you to explain.

And once I get that opening, I say this: "Well, it's just a quick 15-minute call where we show you exactly how other businesses in your industry are currently using AI. And to be transparent, the reason we can do it for free is because we know that 80% of businesses are going to adopt AI in the next 12 to 18 months. So we're just building relationships early now, so if or when the time comes where you do want to adopt AI, you have a contact to reach out to. So does that 15-minute call sound fair sometime when you're available?"

The third possibility is something negative — "I'm not interested." In which case: "Out of curiosity, I mean, is AI on the radar for you guys? Is that something you've explored?" They're either going to hang up or say no or yes. If they say no: "Well, in that case, I actually think the audit would be beneficial to you because all we're looking to do is show you exactly how other businesses in your industry are currently using AI." Then complete the same close.

The key here is to get to the sentence ["does a 15-minute call sound fair?"] The more times you get to this sentence and you go for the ask, the more success you're going to have.

Two things you need to do or else this whole thing falls to crap. The first thing is talk with confidence and certainty — it's not what you say, it's how you say it. The second thing is be consistent. This is a volume game. You can't make 100 calls on Thursday and never touch the phone again. You need to be making X calls per day.

Show rate system: when you book meetings, not everyone's going to show up. Three follow-ups: 24 hours before, an hour before, and then 15 minutes before. And a Loom video — personalized 1-minute video right after booking. I just say their name, I say what we're going to cover, I give them a tidbit of value. 5 minutes of your time has never had so much leverage.

When they show up to the meeting: skip chit-chat. After "hey, how's it going?" — 10 seconds max — I say "I have some stuff prepared for you, but is there anything in particular you wanted to discuss today about AI?" This shows you're not wasting their time and their time is valuable to you. Then present the slide deck. You need control of the conversation.
